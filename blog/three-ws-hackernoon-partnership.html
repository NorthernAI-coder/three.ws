three.ws Teams Up with HackerNoon

three.ws is partnering with HackerNoon to launch a builder-focused content initiative — packed with feature articles, hands-on tutorials, in-depth guides, and developer resources built for those actively shipping with AI.

What we're publishing

The HackerNoon partnership creates a permanent home for three.ws technical writing aimed at developers and AI enthusiasts who are actually building — not just reading about what others are building. Content will cover:

  • How-to tutorials. Step-by-step guides for embedding <agent-3d>, configuring LLM brains, setting up x402 payments, and wiring agents to data feeds.
  • Architecture deep-dives. How the three.ws stack is built — WebGL rendering, Solana minting, ERC-8004 identity, x402 payment rails, and the MCP server.
  • Integration guides. How to wire three.ws agents into DeFi interfaces, e-commerce sites, productivity tools, and anywhere else a 3D AI presence would add value.
  • Builder profiles. Spotlights on developers and projects using three.ws in unexpected or particularly compelling ways.

Why HackerNoon

HackerNoon reaches millions of developers, founders, and technologists who read for signal rather than noise. It's one of the few tech publishing platforms that has maintained credibility with builders across a decade of industry cycles — from Web 2.0 through mobile to AI. That's the audience we want to write for and learn from.
