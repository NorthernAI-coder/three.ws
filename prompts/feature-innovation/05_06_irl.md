## 1. Mission

The end user wants to *leave an agent in the real world* — anchored to a physical spot — and discover the agents other people have left nearby, all without exposing anyone's location on a map. `/irl` is three.ws's location-anchored AR layer: pin your agent to a real floor via camera AR, GPS, and compass; walk around and have nearby agents fade in by proximity (dot → impostor → full mesh) with arrival cues and an interaction sheet. Location is *private* — agents appear only to people physically near them, never on a list or map.

"Gamechanging" here means inventing a genuinely new social primitive: **agents you leave for each other in physical space**, discoverable only by being there. Geocaching meets the agent economy meets AR. The bar is a moment people travel to experience — finding a stranger's agent on a real street corner, leaving a reply, paying it in $THREE, and knowing the next passerby will find what you left. Privacy-first by design.

- **Where it's mediocre, thin, or unfinished:** It's a remarkable engine but the *social/game loop is thin* — you pin and discover, but there's little reason to *return* or to *interact deeply*. Discovery is purely "what's around me now" with no breadcrumbs toward nearby clusters.

## 4. Innovation directions (seeds — you are expected to go beyond them)

- **Leave-a-note / reply threads on a pin.** Make pins conversational drops: anyone nearby can leave a note, an emote, or a $THREE tip on your agent, and you get notified (push/SSE) when they do. The "someone left something for me here" loop is the whole magic — finish it end-to-end with real persistence.
- **Talking agents at the spot.** Wire the placed agent to its character/persona so tapping it opens a short in-AR conversation (the platform already has character configs and worker LLM proxies) — a pinned agent that *responds* is categorically new.
- **Proximity breadcrumbs & "warmer/colder".** Without revealing exact locations, give a directional "agents this way" hint and a heat sense as you approach a cluster — turn the city into a treasure map while preserving the privacy model.
- **Trails & quests.** Creators can chain pins into a route ("follow my agents across the park"); completing a trail unlocks a reward. Geocaching for the agent economy.
- **Away notifications.** When someone views/pays/replies to your pin, notify you (respecting the privacy model) so leaving an agent feels like planting something that grows.
- **Collaborative anchor refinement.** When multiple users pin/observe the same spot, fuse their poses to tighten anchor accuracy over time (`PATCH /api/irl/pins` already exists) — crowd-sourced AR precision.

> These are starting points, not a checklist. The best idea may not be listed — find it. Think second-order: `/irl` connects to characters (talking pins), payments SDK ($THREE drops), `/walk` (shared-floor AR), and agent profiles (the agent-card sheet); trails could surface in `/discover`. **Wire those connections** while never breaking the location-privacy guarantee. The best platforms feel like everything is linked.
