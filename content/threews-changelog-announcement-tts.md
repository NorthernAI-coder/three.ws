three.ws is built in public, and we ship every single day.

Not every week. Not only when there is something big to announce. Every day. New features, new tools, fixes, upgrades, the small things and the large ones. And all of it goes into one place, the changelog, logged in the open with a date stamped on it.

Most projects talk about what they are going to do. A roadmap. A vision. A someday. We do the opposite. We would rather show you what we already shipped than promise you what we might. The changelog is not a marketing page. It is a receipt. A running, public record of real work, day after day, that anyone can scroll back through and verify for themselves.

And the pace is the point. In a span of weeks, three.ws went from a simple idea to a living product. Agents you can build. An economy you can watch move in real time. Three-dimensional tools any creator can use. And partnerships with some of the biggest names in technology. None of that arrived in a single announcement. It arrived one shipped update at a time, in plain sight.

That is what building in public actually means. Not hiding the slow days. Not inflating the big ones. Just steady, visible, accountable progress that you can follow as it happens.

So if you want to understand where this is going, do not wait for the next big thread. Watch the work itself. Every feature. Every release. Every improvement. Dated, public, and real.

Follow the changelog at three dot w s slash changelog. And keep watching, because tomorrow there will be more.
